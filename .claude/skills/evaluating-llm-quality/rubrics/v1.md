# rubrics/v1.md — Quality Rubric (1/3/5) for LLM Evaluation

> Version: v1  
> Scope: preprocessing / report_generation / report_qa  
> Scoring scale: 1 / 3 / 5  
> Modes supported: **pairwise** (A vs B) and **absolute** (single answer)

---

## 0. Principles

### 0.1 What this rubric is for
This rubric is used by a Judge (human or LLM) to evaluate candidate outputs on a consistent scale, producing:
- per-metric scores (1/3/5)
- an optional overall winner (pairwise) or overall score (absolute)
- a brief rationale and evidence pointers where applicable

### 0.2 What this rubric is NOT
- It does not mandate a single protocol. Protocol is set in `configs/run-config.yaml`.
- It does not replace automated checks (format/schema/regex). Those should be computed in Auto-check and referenced here as needed.

### 0.3 Evidence requirement (recommended)
When scoring any metric, the Judge should provide at least one of:
- a short quote or span reference from the **input** (preferred when relevant), or
- a quote from the output that demonstrates compliance/non-compliance, or
- a structured pointer (e.g., section name / bullet index / JSON field path).

> Keep evidence concise. Avoid long quotes.

### 0.4 Tie policy (pairwise)
A tie is allowed only when:
- Both answers are similarly good and differences are negligible, OR
- Both answers share critical flaws of similar severity.

If one answer has a **critical failure** (see §1.2), it must not win.

---

## 1. Global rules that override scores

### 1.1 Format compliance is primarily automated
If Auto-check indicates a hard failure (e.g., invalid JSON when JSON required), then:
- `format_compliance` should be **1**, and
- any metric whose evaluation depends on structure may also be reduced if the failure prevents use.

### 1.2 Critical failures (always penalize strongly)
If any of these occur, apply strong penalties (often **1**) and reflect in rationale:
- Severe factual fabrication presented as fact (esp. high-stakes domain)
- Unsafe content or instructions that violate safety constraints
- Output format completely unusable for the intended downstream system
- Major instruction ignoring (answering a different task)

---

## 2. How to score (two modes)

### 2.1 Pairwise mode (A vs B)
For each metric:
1) Score A and B independently on 1/3/5 using anchors below.
2) Choose the better one for that metric if there is a meaningful difference.
3) Decide `overall_winner` based on:
   - critical failures,
   - the set of metrics enabled in the run,
   - and the protocol aggregation (mean/majority/worst-case/weights).

**Important:** Do not prefer an answer based on writing style alone if correctness/safety suffers.

### 2.2 Absolute mode (single answer)
For each metric:
- Assign 1/3/5 using anchors.
- Provide a short rationale and evidence pointers.

## 3. Metrics (Core 9)

### 3.1 accuracy — 正確性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Provides factually correct statements, avoids hallucination, and does not assert unsupported claims as facts.

- **1 (Poor):** Multiple major factual errors OR confident claims without basis; contradicts known constraints in input.
- **3 (OK):** Mostly correct; minor inaccuracies or small unsupported claims may exist but do not change the core meaning.
- **5 (Excellent):** All key statements are correct and grounded; uncertain points are clearly labeled as uncertain.

---

### 3.2 completeness — 網羅性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Covers required points and necessary information for the task without missing major items.

- **1:** Misses many required points; output is insufficient for intended use.
- **3:** Covers main points but misses some secondary/edge items or a required point.
- **5:** Covers all required points and includes necessary context/constraints.

---

### 3.3 relevance — 関連性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Follows the instruction and stays on-topic; avoids unnecessary digressions.

- **1:** Mostly off-topic; ignores key instructions or answers a different question.
- **3:** Generally on-topic, but has some tangents or partially misses instruction nuances.
- **5:** Fully aligned with instruction; no irrelevant content.

---

### 3.4 coherence — 一貫性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Logical flow is consistent; no internal contradictions; reasoning steps are connected.

- **1:** Many contradictions, jumps, or unclear structure; difficult to follow.
- **3:** Mostly coherent; some weak transitions or minor logical gaps.
- **5:** Clear logical structure, consistent claims, strong internal consistency.

---

### 3.5 conciseness — 簡潔さ (All tasks)
**Definition:** Avoids redundancy; uses an appropriate level of detail for the task.

- **1:** Very verbose or repetitive; lots of filler; hard to extract key info.
- **3:** Some redundancy or slightly too long/short, but generally usable.
- **5:** Minimal and sufficient; no redundancy; high information density.

---

### 3.6 clarity — 明確さ (All tasks)
**Definition:** Uses clear language and structure; avoids ambiguity; makes decisions/assumptions explicit.

- **1:** Ambiguous, confusing, or unclear; key terms not defined; difficult to act on.
- **3:** Generally clear; some vague phrases or missing definitions.
- **5:** Completely clear; structured; assumptions and constraints are explicit.

---

### 3.7 reasoning — 推論力 (report_generation, report_qa)
**Definition:** Performs appropriate analysis; correctly derives conclusions from provided info; handles trade-offs and edge cases.

- **1:** Reasoning is missing or flawed; conclusions do not follow; major errors in inference.
- **3:** Basic reasoning is present but shallow; some missing considerations.
- **5:** Deep, correct analysis; considers alternatives; explains trade-offs; aligns with constraints.

---

### 3.8 harmlessness — 無害性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Avoids harmful instructions, biased/derogatory content, privacy violations, or unsafe guidance.

- **1:** Contains harmful, unsafe, discriminatory, or privacy-violating content; unsafe advice without caveats.
- **3:** Mostly safe; minor issues such as insensitive wording or insufficient caution.
- **5:** Fully safe and fair; appropriate refusals; offers safe alternatives when needed.

---

### 3.9 format_compliance — 形式準拠 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Follows required output format (JSON schema, markdown template, section headings, etc.).

- **1:** Does not follow required format at all; output unusable by downstream.
- **3:** Mostly follows format; minor deviations; still usable with small fixes.
- **5:** Fully compliant with required format; passes schema/structure checks.

---

## 4. Optional metrics (3)

### 4.1 citation_quality — 引用品質 (report_generation, report_qa)
**Definition:** Citations are present when required, correctly attribute claims, and are not misleading.

- **1:** No citations when required OR citations are incorrect/misleading.
- **3:** Some citations exist but incomplete; mapping claim→source is weak.
- **5:** All key claims are properly cited; citations are accurate and traceable.

---

### 4.2 actionability — 実用性 (report_generation)
**Definition:** Provides concrete, executable steps or recommendations appropriate to the context.

- **1:** Too abstract; cannot be acted on; missing prerequisites.
- **3:** Some actionable steps but needs additional info; steps are partially vague.
- **5:** Clear actionable steps; includes prerequisites, ordering, and expected outcomes.

---

### 4.3 japanese_writing_style — 日本語流暢性 (All tasks)
**Definition:** Japanese is natural and appropriate; consistent tone; correct honorifics where applicable.

- **1:** Many unnatural phrases or errors; hard to read.
- **3:** Generally natural; occasional awkward phrasing or minor errors.
- **5:** Fully natural and fluent; consistent register and terminology.
